Project Description:

Older people, accounting for a large proportion of Australian population, are more susceptible to getting sick with COVID-19, and cause higher fatality rate once getting infected compared to youngers. Therefore, providing older people with special protection and care will contribute to the infection control, overall social stability and further development.

Utilizing the available open data and processing data virtualization, we aim to design the solution to mitigate infected information limitation specially faced by older people because they are unfamiliar with digital devise application, optimize medical resources and volunteer allocation, and prioritize the old people’s medical need.

Data Analysis:

Integrating the open data of population aging distribution across main states in Australia, there is no doubt that Australia is facing the aggravating trend of aging population, which is also the serious threat of fighting COVID-19. Focusing on the older people infected condition, we collected the COVID-19 case and COVID-19 death data and calculated the death rate on the nine divided parts of age levels, from which we realized that although the infected ratio of older people is relatively low, the death rate is high because of poor physical quality in the equal lacking vaccine condition.

Therefore, effectively settling down the older people’s medical needs will hopefully decrease the death case and further benefit the pandemic confinement and control. After comparing the available bed and relevant medical resources with infected case, we concluded that the current medical resource is scarce, only efficiently allocating the resources can older people get effective treatments.

To achieve this, we input the location data of medical institution and real-time available beds in users’ GPS position scope, screen the unavailable resource, and automate the process of the best medical institution selections, which will provide valuable help for older people to lock the destination.

Also, WeCare classifies the areas into High Risk, Medium Risk and Low Risk based on analyzing tracking data of the suspected case and confirmed case. Moreover, considering older people and medical institutions may need volunteers for different purposes like general enquiry and professional medical support, WeCare realizes the consistent match to consolidate the volunteer allocation structure based on analyzing and processing the data of demand and supply.

Data analysis lays a solid foundation for the whole solution process, during which we found that data can produce a marked effect for a larger extent with elaborate ideas and technical support.

What WeCare do?

We input the location data of medical institution and real-time available beds in users’ GPS position scope, screen the unavailable resource, and automate the process of the best medical institution selections, which will provide valuable help for older people to lock the destination.

Generally, WeCare will count the sum of infected people in real-time for selected state, record the infected people’s trajectory to avoid the potential contagion, accelerate the medical resources geolocation, direct the older people to the nearest available medical institution with the reported number of available beds. Moreover, treating older people as key users, WeCare develops various functions to make it accessible and convenient for them. First of all, WeCare simplifies the operating process combining with data virtualization, which is easy for old people to understand and operate. Second, WeCare bridges the effective connection between old people and medical institutions, make a direct call or send the message with specific requests which can be easily selected in the prepared list to the medical institutions and be marked as “Urgent” to provide exceptional attention for older people. Third, users and medical institutions can get in touch with volunteers in timely manner considering the location and volunteering purpose which could be matched with corresponding professional expertise, in order to optimize volunteer allocation structure.

Also, WeCare classifies the areas into High Risk, Medium Risk and Low Risk based on analyzing tracking data of the suspected case and confirmed case. Moreover, considering older people and medical institutions may need volunteers for different purposes like general enquiry and professional medical support, WeCare could helper the elderly to connect to nearby volunteers.

Data analysis lays a solid foundation for the whole solution process, during which we found that data can produce a marked effect for a larger extent with elaborate ideas and technical support.
